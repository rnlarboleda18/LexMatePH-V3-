--- RULE 132 SECTION 19 ---
'For the purpose of their presentation in evidence, documents are either public or private.  \n\nPublic documents are:  \n    (a) The written official acts, or records of the sovereign authority, official bodies and tribunals, and public officers, whether of the Philippines, or of a foreign country;  \n    (b) Documents acknowledged before a notary public except last wills and testaments;  \n    (c) Documents that are considered public documents under treaties and conventions which are in force between the Philippines and the country of source; and  \n    (d) Public records, kept in the Philippines, of private documents required by law to be entered therein.  \n\nAll other writings are private.'

--- RULE 18 SECTION 2 ---
"The pre-trial is mandatory and should be terminated promptly. The court shall consider:  \n\n    (a) The possibility of an amicable settlement or of a submission to alternative modes of dispute resolution;  \n    (b) The simplification of the issues;  \n    (c) The possibility of obtaining stipulations or admissions of facts and of documents to avoid unnecessary proof;  \n    (d) The limitation of the number and identification of witnesses and the setting of trial dates;  \n    (e) The advisability of a preliminary reference of issues to a commissioner;  \n    (f) The propriety of rendering judgment on the pleadings, or summary judgment, or of dismissing the action should a valid ground therefor be found to exist;  \n    (g) The requirement for the parties to:  \n\n        1. Mark their respective evidence if not yet marked in the judicial affidavits of their witnesses;  \n        2. Examine and make comparisons of the adverse parties' evidence vis-a-vis the copies to be marked;  \n        3. Manifest for the record stipulations regarding the faithfulness of the reproductions and the genuineness and due execution of the adverse parties' evidence;  \n        4. Reserve evidence not available at the pre-trial, but only in the following manner:  \n\n            i. For testimonial evidence, by giving the name or position and the nature of the testimony of the proposed witness;  \n            ii. For documentary evidence and other object evidence, by giving a particular description of the evidence.  \n\n        No reservation shall be allowed if not made in the manner described above.  \n\n    (h) Such other matters as may aid in the prompt disposition of the action.  \n\nThe failure without just cause of a party and counsel to appear during pre-trial, despite notice, shall result in a waiver of any objections to the faithfulness of the reproductions marked, or their genuineness and due execution.  \n\nThe failure without just cause of a party and/or counsel to bring the evidence required shall be deemed a waiver of the presentation of such evidence.  \n\nThe branch clerk of court shall prepare the minutes of the pre-trial, which shall have the following format: (See prescribed form)"

--- RULE 18 SECTION 7 ---
"Upon termination of the pre-trial, the court shall issue an order within ten (10) calendar days which shall recite in detail the matters taken up. The order shall include:  \n\n    (a) An enumeration of the admitted facts;  \n    (b) The minutes of the pre-trial conference;  \n    (c) The legal and factual issue/s to be tried;  \n    (d) The applicable law, rules, and jurisprudence;  \n    (e) The evidence marked;  \n    (f) The specific trial dates for continuous trial, which shall be within the period provided by the Rules;  \n    (g) The case flowchart to be determined by the court, which shall contain the different stages of the proceedings up to the promulgation of the decision and the use of time frames for each stage in setting the trial dates;  \n    (h) A statement that the one-day examination of witness rule and most important witness rule under A.M. No. 03-1-09-SC (Guidelines for Pre-Trial) shall be strictly followed; and  \n    (i) A statement that the court shall render judgment on the pleadings or summary judgment, as the case may be.  \n\nThe direct testimony of witnesses for the plaintiff shall be in the form of judicial affidavits. After the identification of such affidavits, cross-examination shall proceed immediately.  \n\nPostponement of presentation of the parties' witnesses at a scheduled date is prohibited, except if it is based on acts of God, force majeure or duly substantiated physical inability of the witness to appear and testify. The party who caused the postponement is warned that the presentation of its evidence must still be terminated within the remaining dates previously agreed upon.  \n\nShould the opposing party fail to appear without valid cause stated in the next preceding paragraph, the presentation of the scheduled witness will proceed with the absent party being deemed to have waived the right to interpose objection and conduct cross-examination.  \n\nThe contents of the pre-trial order shall control the subsequent proceedings, unless modified before trial to prevent manifest injustice."

--- RULE 13 SECTION 3 ---
'The filing of pleadings and other court submissions shall be made by:  \n\n    (a) Submitting personally the original thereof, plainly indicated as such, to the court;  \n    (b) Sending them by registered mail;  \n    (c) Sending them by accredited courier; or  \n    (d) Transmitting them by electronic mail or other electronic means as may be authorized by the court in places where the court is electronically equipped.  \n\nIn the first case, the clerk of court shall endorse on the pleading the date and hour of filing. In the second and third cases, the date of the mailing of motions, pleadings, and other court submissions, and payments or deposits, as shown by the post office stamp on the envelope or the registry receipt, shall be considered as the date of their filing, payment, or deposit in court. The envelope shall be attached to the record of the case. In the fourth case, the date of electronic transmission shall be considered as the date of filing.'
