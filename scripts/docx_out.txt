Rule 73 & 74: Settlement of EstateDescription: Republic Act No. 11576 (2021) amended the jurisdictional thresholds originally set by BP 129 and RA 7691. This update accounts for inflation and court decongestion by shifting higher-value estates to the first-level courts.Effect: The exclusive original jurisdiction for probate proceedings (testate and intestate) is now divided as follows:MTC/MeTC/MTCC: Gross value of the estate does not exceed ₱2,000,000.RTC: Gross value of the estate exceeds ₱2,000,000.Note: Rule 74, Section 2's specific mention of a ₱10,000 limit for summary settlement is considered superseded by these general jurisdictional increases.Rules 92–97: GuardianshipDescription: The Supreme Court promulgated A.M. Jurisdiction is transferred to Family Courts.Incompetent Adults: Rules 92–97 remain the governing procedure for adults who are hospitalized lepers, prodigals, or of unsound mind. The period to appeal is 30 days (instead of the standard 15 days for a Notice of Appeal) because of the requirement to submit a full record of the proceedings.
